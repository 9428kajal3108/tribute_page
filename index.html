Biography of Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata (born December 28, 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was the chairman of the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts. In 2008, he received the Padma Vibhushan, the second highest civilian honour in India, after receiving the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian honour in India in 2000. Ratan Tata was born into a wealthy Parsi family in Mumbai, India. His grandfather, Jamsetji Tata, was the founder of the Tata Group, one of India's largest conglomerates. Ratan Tata studied architecture at Cornell University in the United States and then returned to India to work for the Tata Group. He held a variety of positions in the company, including chairman of Tata Steel and Tata Motors. In 1991, he was appointed chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. As chairman of the Tata Group, Ratan Tata oversaw a period of significant growth and expansion. He acquired several new businesses, including Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel. He also launched several new initiatives, such as the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car. Ratan Tata was known for his commitment to social responsibility and his focus on innovation. He was also a strong advocate for corporate governance and transparency. Ratan Tata retired as chairman of the Tata Group in 2012. He was succeeded by Cyrus Mistry. However, Mistry was ousted from the company in 2016 and Ratan Tata was appointed interim chairman. He stepped down as interim chairman in February 2017 and was succeeded by N. Chandrasekaran. Ratan Tata is a respected figure in India and around the world. He has been praised for his business acumen, his commitment to social responsibility, and his focus on innovation. He is a role model for many young people in India and he is an inspiration to entrepreneurs around the world.
